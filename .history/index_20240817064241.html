Semantic HTML:

Semantic HTML uses elements that describe the content they contain. For example,
using header for the introductory content at the top of a page, footer for the content at the bottom,
article for independent, self-contained content, and section for grouping related content.


Accessibility:

Semantic elements provide meaning to screen readers and other assistive technologies,
making your content more accessible to users with disabilities.


Search Engine Optimization:

Search engines like Google understand and prioritize content marked up with semantic HTML.
Using semantic elements can improve your website's search engine rankings


Clarity and Structure:

Semantic HTML makes your code easier to read and maintain.
It helps other developers understand your code and your intentions.


footer:

Contains footer information like copyright, contact details, etc.
It helps in structuring the page and providing additional context.
Search engines may consider footer content for indexing and ranking.


article:

Represents a self-contained piece of content that can be distributed and reused independently.
It helps screen readers identify main content areas.
Search engines may prioritize article content for indexing.
